<Accordion title="March 13, 2026 — Updated Discovery Bounds Methodology (v2)">

We upgraded the discovery bounds methodology, starting with CL (WTIOIL). The current v2 methodology is described in the [Discovery Bounds](/perp-mechanics/external-price) page. The following is an archive of the v1 discovery bounds design with which CL was launched.

### v1 Design: How It Worked

Discovery Bounds were leverage-based limits that restricted how far the mark price could deviate from the external oracle price. Enabled by default on all markets, these bounds provided clearly defined price levels within which market makers and other market participants could confidently participate, without risk of liquidation. Their purpose was to prevent price movements that exceeded what could reasonably constitute true price discovery.

**Bound Range**

The mark price was restricted to move within `1 / max leverage` of the last externally derived oracle price. Discovery Bounds were enforced 24/7, but were particularly relevant during internal pricing sessions (e.g., weekends or holidays), in which the external reference price remained unchanged for extended periods. Market participants could use this information to manage their risk and margin accordingly.

**Example**

If the Silver oracle price was $75 at Friday's 17:00 ET close, given 25× max leverage, the applicable Discovery Bound was ±4%. As a result, both the mark price and oracle price were restricted to the range $72–$78 until external pricing resumed on Sunday at 18:00 ET.

If Silver was trading above Friday's last externally-derived fair price and a trader believed the move did not reflect fair value, they could enter a short position. Once external pricing resumed, the market could reprice toward the externally-derived reference price depending on prevailing market conditions. Importantly, if a trader's liquidation price lay outside the active price bounds, their position could not be liquidated while those bounds were in effect.

<Accordion title="February 19, 2026 — Updated Index Oracle Methodology (v2 for XYZ100)">

We upgraded the oracle derivation for equity indices, applicable to XYZ100. The current v2 methodology is described in the [Equity Indices](/asset-directory/equity-indices) section of the documentation. The following is an archive of the v1 oracle design with which XYZ100 was launched.

### v1 Design: External Price Derivation

For equity indices such as XYZ100, the relayer consumed executable quotes for the underlying traditional (dated) equity index futures from institutional liquidity providers. This provided robust external pricing nearly 23 hours per day, five days per week, extending external coverage beyond daytime cash sessions.

The futures quote was adjusted to an implied spot value which was then included as the oracle price in a relayer update to HyperCore. The conversion from futures to spot followed the cost-of-carry model (spot–futures parity):

```
S = F × e^(−(r−q)T)
```

where `S` is the spot (oracle) price, `F` is the externally-sourced dated-futures price, `T` is the time to settlement (in years), `r` is the interest rate, and `q` is the forward dividend yield. The discount rate `d = (r−q)` took into account the prevailing borrow rates as well as the forward dividend yield of the underlying, and could be updated periodically in accordance with changing market conditions.

### Worked Example: Deriving the XYZ100 Oracle Price

The following example shows how to independently replicate and verify an oracle price using the v1 methodology.

From the equity index calendar, the active contract suffix is Z5, with an expiration time of `2025-12-19 13:30:00 UTC`.

The Pyth NMZ5 feed is reporting a price of `$24,904.2` at the current time, `2025-10-14 17:06:05 UTC`.

The time to expiry is `0.180285473` years.

At the time of writing, the discount rate `r−q = 4.0%`. Plugging these values into the equation:

- `F = 24904.2`
- `r−q = 0.04`
- `T = 0.180285473`

We calculate `S = 24725.25`. The derived spot price is **$24,725.25**.

<Accordion title="December 19, 2025 — Funding Rate Formula Update">

We reduced the funding rate across all XYZ markets by applying a scaling factor of 0.5 to Hyperliquid's funding rate formula.

**Background**

The default funding rate for crypto perpetuals has remained unchanged at 0.01% per 8 hours since BitMEX first introduced the mechanism in 2016. Borrow rates for traditional asset classes such as equities and commodities are typically closer to SOFR + 1–2%.

**Result**

Applying a 0.5 scaling factor lowers the baseline funding rate to approximately 5.5% annualized. It also results in less aggressive funding during weekend price discovery windows.

**Formula**

```
Funding Rate XYZ (F) = 0.5 × [Average Premium Index (P) + clamp(interest rate − P, −0.0005, 0.0005)]
```

</Accordion>
