A fundamental feature of trade[XYZ] is 24/7 price discovery. When external markets close, XYZ traders continue to buy, sell, and speculate on an asset's fair price — making XYZ the primary venue for price discovery over weekends, holidays, and other extended sessions. Discovery bounds make this possible in a controlled and predictable way by limiting how far the mark price can move from a reference price. By capping the extent of price moves, market makers and traders can participate in extended-hours price discovery with well-defined risk. If your liquidation price falls outside the active bounds, your position cannot be liquidated while those bounds remain in effect.

## How Discovery Bounds Work

### Instantaneous Bounds

Discovery bounds restrict the mark price to within **±(1 / max leverage)** of a reference price, during both external and internal pricing sessions. For example, WTIOIL has a 20× maximum leverage, so its instantaneous discovery bound is ±5% around the reference price.

During **external sessions**, the reference price is the last externally-derived fair price. During **internal sessions** (e.g., weekends or holidays), the reference price starts at the last externally derived oracle price — the Friday close for assets that follow weekday market hours. For Pre-IPO Perpetual markets without an external price, the initial reference price is the XYZ-defined starting reference for that market.

### Re-Anchoring (Ratcheting)

A static ±5% bound around Friday's close would prevent the market from reflecting genuine large moves over a weekend. The ratcheting mechanism solves this.

When the oracle price approaches the edge of the current bound and reaches a configured **trigger threshold** — for example, 90% of the distance from the reference price to the upper bound for WTIOIL — the reference price **re-anchors** to that bound, and a fresh bound is established around the new reference price. This allows the market to reflect genuine moves during extended periods without external data, rather than freezing at a static limit.

#### Trigger Formulas

```text
upper_trigger = reference_price × (1 + discovery_bound × oracle_threshold)
```

```text
lower_trigger = reference_price × (1 - discovery_bound × oracle_threshold)
```

### Resets and Hard Caps

Each market has a configured number of permitted re-anchors per direction, called **resets**, specified independently for upward and downward movement. The counter for each direction increments by one each time a new bound and trigger is calculated from a reference price.

Once the configured maximum number of resets is reached, the bound becomes a **hard cap** — no further movement in that direction is permitted until external pricing resumes. When external pricing resumes, the reference price reverts to the live externally-derived price and all reset counters return to zero.

A market with zero resets configured behaves as a static bound around the external close price.

<Note>
  The number of resets per market is specified on a per-asset basis. You can find the values for each asset in the [Specification Index](/resources/specification-index) and [Specification Index (IPOPs)](/resources/specification-index-ipops).
</Note>

## Example: WTIOIL

The following example uses these parameters:

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Discovery Bound | ±5% (from 20× max leverage) |
| Oracle Threshold | 90% |
| Friday Closing External Price (initial reference) | $100 |

### Upward Path

| Level | Reference Price | Lower Bound | Upper Bound | Tradeable Range | Upper Trigger | Lower Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | 100.00 | 95.00 | 105.00 | 95.00 – 105.00 | 104.50 | 95.50 |
| 1 | 105.00 | 99.75 | 110.25 | 99.75 – 110.25 | 109.73 | 100.28 |
| 2 (hard cap) | 110.25 | 104.74 | 115.76 | 104.74 – 115.76 | N/A | N/A |

### Step-by-Step Walkthrough

1. The mark price trades within the Level 0 range of 95.00 – 105.00.
2. Over Saturday, the oracle price rises on genuine buying pressure and reaches **104.50** (the upper trigger: `100.00 × 1.045`).
3. **Re-anchor:** the reference price moves to 105.00 (the upper bound). Upward level counter = 1.
4. New tradeable range: 99.75 – 110.25. The oracle price continues from where it was (~104.50) with room to rise further.
5. Buying continues. The oracle price reaches **109.73** (the new upper trigger: `105.00 × 1.045`).
6. **Re-anchor:** the reference price moves to 110.25. Upward level counter = 2.
7. New tradeable range: 104.74 – 115.76. This is the final level upward (hard cap reached).
8. If the oracle price reaches 115.76, it hits the hard cap. No further upward movement is permitted until external pricing resumes.

```text
Maximum price from origin = 100.00 × 1.05^3 ≈ 115.76  (+15.76%)
Maximum price below origin = 100.00 × 0.95^3 ≈ 85.74  (-14.26%)
```

**Reversal:** The mechanism is fully bidirectional. If the oracle price reverses and hits the lower trigger, the reference price re-anchors downward using the same logic. Upward and downward level counters are **independent** — ratcheting up does not consume downward levels.

## Effect in Practice

- **Broader price discovery during genuine events.** The discoverable range expands beyond the instantaneous bound to a wider total range that depends on the configured number of resets — for example, from ±5% to approximately ±15.8% for WTIOIL — allowing the market to reflect large moves rather than freezing at the static limit.

- **The instantaneous range is static.** The tiers shift the window; they do not widen it. At any given moment, the mark price can only move within the instantaneous range (±5% for WTIOIL) around the current reference price.

- **The window re-centers as it moves.** After an upward ratchet, the floor rises. The system commits to the move by shifting the entire window upward, which constrains reversal amplitude and reduces re-open gap risk.

- **Levels are independent per direction.** Upward ratchets do not consume downward budget and vice versa.

- **Risk management.** Positions can only be liquidated if the liquidation price falls within the active bounds. Outside of those bounds, liquidation is suspended until the bounds move or external pricing resumes.

<Tip>
  Manage your risk based on the **maximum possible range** for an asset, not just the instantaneous range. For WTIOIL, the maximum discoverable move from the external close is approximately ±15.8%, even though the instantaneous bound at any given moment is only ±5%.
</Tip>
