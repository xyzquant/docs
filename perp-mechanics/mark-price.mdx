The mark price is the price that matters most to your open positions. trade[XYZ] uses the mark price to calculate margin requirements, determine liquidation thresholds, trigger stop and limit orders, and compute your unrealized P&L. Because it draws on multiple independent inputs, the mark price is resistant to manipulation from any single data source.

## How the Mark Price Is Calculated

The mark price is the **median of three components**:

1. **The oracle price.** The externally-derived or EMA-advanced oracle described on the [Oracle Price](/perp-mechanics/oracle-price) page.

2. **Oracle price + EMA of basis.** The oracle price plus a 150-second continuous-time exponentially weighted moving average of the difference between the perpetual's mid-price and the oracle price. This component tracks how far the perpetual is trading relative to the oracle, smoothed over time.

3. **Local market mid.** The median of the best bid, best ask, and last trade price, computed directly from the order book.

## Who Computes Each Component

The Relayer publishes components **(1)** and **(2)** for every asset in each update. The Hyperliquid protocol (HyperCore) independently computes component **(3)** from live order book data, then takes the median of all three components to produce the final mark price.

Using the median of three independent sources ensures that a single outlier — whether a stale oracle, a thin order book, or a momentary spread spike — cannot unilaterally move your mark price.

## Update Clamping

To protect against data anomalies, Relayer updates for both the mark price and the oracle price are clamped to ±50 basis points of the current value.

<Note>
  The ±50 bps clamp applies per Relayer update. This prevents sudden large price jumps from immediately propagating to your margin or P&L, giving the system time to reflect genuine price moves rather than data anomalies.
</Note>
